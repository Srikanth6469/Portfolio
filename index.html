SENTIMENT ANALYSIS FOR MICROBLOGS

Twitter is a microblogging website that enables users to express themselves on various topics, events, products, and services through a sharing and conversation platform. A general taxonomy of tweets can be developed based on their relevance to a specific topic searched for. Several machine learning techniques are actively employed to classify tweets into positive, negative, and neutral groups based on their sentiments. For instance, baseline and Naive Bayes Classifier are commonly used methods. In this study, we address the sentiment classification challenge on the Twitter dataset. We employ a combination of text preprocessing techniques and the Naive Bayes classifier to perform sentiment analysis.

Online retail selling system

The project is a web-based retail application system for an existing shop. The project objective is to deliver the online retail application to the Android platform. This project aims to provide the advantages of online retail to customers of physical shops. Consequently, customers can access the services of online shopping and home delivery from their preferred shop. This system can be implemented in any local shop or multi-national branded shops with retail outlet chains. If shops are providing an online portal where customers can enjoy shopping from anywhere, they will not lose any more customers to the trending online shops like Flipkart or eBay. Since the application is accessible on smartphones, it is easily available and always accessible.
